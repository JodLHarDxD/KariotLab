The practice
of brand,
reconsidered.

A short, signed document on what we’re done tolerating, and what we’re building instead. Read in seven minutes. Quote freely.

Somewhere in the last fifteen years, the practice of brand became a sixty-page PDF. The sixty-page PDF became a deliverable. The deliverable became a file in a folder. And the file in the folder became, with depressing reliability, the last time anyone touched the brand on purpose.

That isn’t how brand is supposed to work. A brand is supposed to argue for itself. It is supposed to be defended by the people who own it — daily, in small choices, in the words on a button and the kerning of a wordmark and the tone of a customer support email at 11pm on a Tuesday.

What the industry sells, mostly, is decoration. What we are building is a different thing entirely.

What we are done tolerating.

We are done with brand projects that finish at launch. We are done with strategy decks that arrive in PowerPoint and never leave it. We are done with $150,000 invoices for what amounts to a logo and a vibe. We are done with the implication that taste is rare, slow, and expensive — when in fact the rare and expensive thing is the discipline to defend a position over time.

A brand is not what you make.
It is what you are willing to defend.

And we are done, particularly, with the lie that founders should outsource their brand. Founders are the people closest to the work. They have the strongest opinions, the sharpest taste, and the most to lose. They should be in the room. They are the room.

What we are building.

Lumis is creative intelligence. Trained on a decade of award-winning work — Cannes, D&AD, Brand New, Type Directors, ADC. Tagged for why the work moves people, not just that it does. Built to argue alongside you, not to execute for you.

You bring the thesis. Lumis brings the discipline. Together, in hours not months, you produce a brand system that has been examined from a hundred angles and decided on purpose. Not a moodboard. Not a deck. A defended position.

The work that matters is the work you can defend.

And then — and this is the part most people miss — the brand stays alive. As you raise, pivot, hire, expand, simplify, the system updates with you. Equity preserved. Drift recognized. Decisions versioned. The brand becomes a living artifact, not a frozen file.

Industry standard vs. Lumis

What the industry sells

  • A logo, a deck, and a moodboard
  • Six months from kickoff to launch
  • $80,000 — $300,000+ for a single brand cycle
  • One identity, frozen at launch
  • Strategy that lives in PowerPoint
  • The founder watches from the sidelines

What Lumis is

  • A defended position, with system to enforce it
  • Hours from brief to working artifact
  • $49 — $249/mo. Cancel any time. Own the output.
  • A living system that evolves with you
  • Strategy that lives in the code, the copy, the kerning
  • The founder is in the room, always

Six principles,
signed at the studio door.

01

The brand is the argument, not the artifact.

The logo is downstream of the position. We build the position first, then dress it in the right marks.

// Strategy
02

Taste is discipline, not magic.

What looks like taste from the outside is, on the inside, a thousand small refusals. We have read the work. We know which refusals to make.

// Craft
03

Speed is a feature of clarity, not a tradeoff.

The reason brand work takes six months isn’t that the work is hard. It’s that the politics are. Lumis removes the politics.

// Tempo
04

A system that doesn’t change is a system that’s dead.

Living brands evolve. Frozen ones rot. We build for evolution, with provenance, versioning, and equity carried forward.

// Living
05

The founder belongs in the room.

You are closest to the work. Your taste is the input most worth listening to. Lumis sits next to you, never in front.

// Authorship
06

Don’t ship a brand you can’t defend.

If you can’t articulate why this color, this word, this type, this tone — the brand is decoration. Ship the defended one.

// Discipline
