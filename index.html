02 / 06   ·   Manifesto

A short note on what we're done tolerating, and what we're building instead.

The industry sells templates, moodboards, and brand books nobody reads.

It charges $150,000 for what amounts to a logo and a deck.

It calls a sixty-page PDF a "system".

We think a brand is a living thing. It should argue for itself, change with its company, and earn its place in culture — not decorate a slide.

Lumis is what happens when a decade of award-winning practice is taught to think alongside you, not for you.

— Signed, the studio Lumis, vol. 4
03 / 06   ·   Process

Three stages. Each is a journey, not a deliverable.

01

Brief
in dialogue.

Tell Lumis what you're building. It will read between the lines, surface tensions you hadn't named, and give you a strategic foothold to argue from.

Time · ~12 min Output · Strategy doc
▸building a coffee subscription for designers, premium feel → position around "taste, not caffeine" → reference: Aesop, Verve, Blue Bottle → tension: premium vs. ritual ▸tone should feel quiet, considered → register: curatorial, not loud → vocabulary: "selected" not "best" ▸

Generate
the system.

Wordmark, palette, type, voice rules, and component primitives — generated in lockstep, evaluated against the brief, refined until they argue for the brand instead of just decorating it.
